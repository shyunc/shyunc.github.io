You were my first experience with grief, or what I think is grief. I'm still unsure.
Is it normal to cry about someone's passing 2 years after it happened? That's odd,
right? I don't know if this website is for me. But, I do remember feeling incredibly
bitter at the world. At everyone who contributed to another spike in co-vid cases
through thoughtless actions. I thought, maybe, if everyone were responsible to a
degree, I could have visited you a lot sooner. A lot sooner.

I didn't know what to say to you when I finally visited. Or if you could understand
me in a tongue foreign to you, but too familiar to me. I wish I knew more to say to
you and I wish I said it much, much sooner. There were plastic flowers. There were
grievers. There were cries, but not from us. The fan was loud.

I wonder a lot about you. If you passed peacefully. If you had any regrets. If you
wished to see anyone one more time, if you knew. If I could have been one of them.
The letters you wrote, what you thought at night. I think wonder a lot, but I found
that I don't have an awful lot to say. Is that terrible?
